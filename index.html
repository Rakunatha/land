Digital Justice & AI in Judiciary

Empirical Study on Public Awareness & Accessibility

By RAKUNATHA KHRISHANTH MANATHRA

Abstract

This study evaluates awareness and usage of e-Judiciary services at the Madras High Court and explores AI integration in judicial processes.

Findings show high awareness of basic tools but low adoption of advanced systems like e-filing and AI.

Key Objectives

• Assess awareness of digital court services

• Evaluate usage & perception

• Identify barriers & suggest improvements

Introduction

Indian judiciary is transitioning from paper-based systems to digital platforms including:

• e-Filing

• Virtual Hearings

• Case Tracking

Problem Statement

Availability ≠ Accessibility

Major gap exists due to:

• Digital illiteracy

• Language barriers

• Lack of awareness

Methodology

• Sample: 200 respondents (Chennai)

• Method: Questionnaire

• Analysis: SPSS, graphs, comparative charts

Key Findings

• High awareness: Case status & cause lists

• Low usage: e-Filing & AI tools

• Major barrier: Digital literacy

Demographic Insights

• Youth → High usage

• Elderly → Low awareness

• Women → Lower participation

Challenges

• Technical difficulty

• Fear of mistakes

• Internet access issues

• Lack of guidance

Role of AI

AI can improve:

• Case management

• Legal research

• Language translation

Global Comparison

India vs US/UK/Canada:

• India → Free access but low awareness

• Developed countries → High usage, better literacy

Recommendations

• Help desks in courts

• Training programs

• Regional language support

• AI integration

Conclusion

Digital justice is powerful but incomplete.

Success depends on awareness, inclusivity & accessibility.
